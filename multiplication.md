Multiplying two integers means adding one number to itself the number of times indicated by the other number (or combining equal groups).

Example:

3 times 4 = 12

This means:
4 + 4 + 4 = 12

With negative numbers:

(-2) \times 5 = -10
A negative times a positive is negative.

(-2) \times (-3) = 6
A negative times a negative is positive.
